What the collaborative model adds — beyond the single-user strategy
Role-aware task routing
Tasks are explicitly owned — mapping screens route to ProAdvisors, business approval screens route to owners. No task is presented to the wrong person.
Save-and-share at every step
Any screen can be paused, exported as a pre-filled review package, and shared via a secure link. No migration requires a single synchronous session.
Cross-client mapping templates
A ProAdvisor who has migrated one client's payroll builds a reusable mapping profile — subsequent clients with similar payroll structures reuse it with one-click confirmation.
Structured handoff receipts
Every handoff between ProAdvisor and business owner generates a dated receipt — who sent what, what decision was requested, and what was confirmed. Full audit trail, no ambiguity.
ProAdvisor dashboard
A single view of all in-progress client migrations — status, pending actions, blockers, and estimated completion. The ProAdvisor never needs to ask "where are we?" for any client.
Async-first by design
The workflow is designed for asynchronous execution. Business owner and ProAdvisor do not need to be in the same session — or even the same timezone — to complete migration.

Still-Overlooked Concerns

Even with the collaborative model in place and both mapping screens confirmed, these concerns persist. They require deliberate design decisions before wireframing begins.

Critical — design before wire
Pay History timeline anchor must be permanent
The visual distinction between migrated (QBDT) and live (QBO) paychecks must persist forever in Pay History — not just as a one-time migration notice. Six months post-migration, an auditor needs to know instantly which records came from where. Badge, divider, and provenance label are permanent design decisions.
Critical — design before wire
Rollback path must be a defined UX state
Neither document defines rollback. Once bank transactions are suppressed and native paychecks are created, rolling back requires reversing both. The BO must know — before migration begins — whether rollback is possible, what it means, and what the window is. "Can I undo this?" must have a designed answer.
Critical — compliance risk
Voided and amended paychecks must be explicitly included
If only the original paycheck migrates without its void reversal, YTD totals are overstated. The pay history configuration screen needs a specific "include voided and amended paychecks" confirmation — with a plain-English explanation of why this is required for W-2 accuracy.
Watch — surfaces in QA
Mid-pay-period migration timing gate
If a customer migrates mid-period — Wednesday of a bi-weekly cycle — the in-progress payroll in QBDT must be completed first. The pre-migration flow needs an explicit gate: "Is there a payroll currently in progress in QBDT?" with a hard block until the answer is no.
Watch — surfaces in QA
Company-side payroll items (FUTA, SUTA, employer FICA) are out of scope in both mapping screens
Employer-side payroll items affect the Chart of Accounts. If the mapping screen covers only employee-facing pay items, the company-side data silently migrates as generic journal entries — recreating the exact problem the PRD is trying to solve, just on the employer side.
Watch — IES segment specific
Mapping template portability across PA firm's client base
A PA managing 20 clients with similar payroll structures should not rebuild the mapping from scratch for each one. The cross-client mapping template must handle: same-industry defaults, custom item variations per client, and a diff-from-template view that shows only what changed — not the full mapping again.
Design decision needed
What happens when the BO ignores the review link?
The collaborative model depends on the BO responding to review requests. When they don't — and they will not, for some percentage of clients — the PA needs escalation paths: automatic reminders, a PA-override option (with audit log), and a clear definition of when migration is allowed to proceed without BO response.
Design decision needed
Idempotency — every action must be safely re-runnable
If migration fails halfway and restarts, the system must produce the same output. The resume migration path must show PA exactly which paychecks already migrated and which still need to run — not a blank-slate restart that risks duplicating records already posted to the HistoricalPaycheck API.
Design Principles — Collaborative Model
P1
Route complexity to competence
Payroll taxonomy, API errors, and mapping edge cases go to ProAdvisors. Business period selection, authorisation, and decommission decisions go to Business Owners. No screen presents both types of decision to the same person simultaneously.
P2
Every handoff generates a receipt
Every time responsibility moves between PA and BO, a dated receipt is generated — who sent what, what decision was requested, and what was confirmed. Ambiguity about "who said what" is a support call. Receipts eliminate it.
P3
Async-first — no synchronous session required
PA and BO do not need to be in the same room, the same session, or even the same timezone to complete migration. Save-and-share at every step. Time-limited review links. Automatic reminders. The workflow accommodates real-world scheduling constraints.
P4
The ProAdvisor is the primary user — design for their scale
A PA managing 20 clients simultaneously cannot afford a per-client setup overhead. Cross-client templates, a single-view dashboard, and bulk resolution across clients are not nice-to-haves — they are the primary design surface for the IES segment.
P5
Irreversible actions require named, dated co-signatures
Migration authorisation, decommission confirmation, and sign-off are irreversible. Each requires a named, dated co-signature from both PA and BO — not a generic checkbox. The signature is the audit record. "I, [PA name], confirm this migration is complete" carries legal weight that "confirm" does not.
P6
Historical and live paychecks are permanently differentiated
The Pay History timeline anchor — visual divider, provenance badge, migration date label — is a permanent design decision on the Pay History screen. It is not a transient notification. Six months after migration, an auditor must be able to identify provenance at a glance, without reading metadata.
